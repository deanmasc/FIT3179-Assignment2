Education in Australia (2023)

What are the Current Enrollment Rates in Educational Programs?

This choropleth map shows the percentage of Australians by gender currently enrolled in different levels of education, from primary to tertiary education.

We can see from this map that Northern Territory and South East Australia have high enrollment %'s, whilst Western Australia and Tasmania have far lower rates.

What is the Growth in Non-School Qualifications?

This area chart depicts the growth in Non-School Qualifications over 2014-2023. The area chart is sectioned by different age ranges and the desired gender can be selected, to show the growth of Non-School Qualifications across demographics.

We can see that across all genders from 2014 there has been significant growth in Non-School Qualifications. This growth is also consistent across all age ranges, suggesting that popularity and utility of Non-School Qualifications has increased.

Difference of Employment Status by Education Level and Gender

This doubled-sided bar chart visualizes the employment status of Australians based on their highest level of education with Males on the right and Females on the left, illustrating the correlation between education and employment status.

We can see that males predominantly work full-time, especially those who have some sort of education qualification, whereas it is more popular for Females to work part-time which could be the result of numerous factors.

How Many Australians Have at Least High School Education?

This map displays the percentage of Australians who have completed at least high school education across various states, through a proportional symbol map. It highlights educational attainment levels nationwide.

We can see that regions like Northern Terriorory and ACT have high percentages of at least high school education levels, whereas states like New South Wales and South Australia have lower rates.

What are each States Most Popular Highest Education?

This bubble chart shows the percentage of total population for a range of education levels depicting the highest education level a person in that state holds, therefore visualising which highest levels of education are most prominent in each state.

We can see that Bachelor degrees are consistently the most popular among each state as the highest level of education. Additionally, ACT has a relatively large proportion of Postgraduate degrees in comparison to the other states.

What Are Popular Fields of Study?

This grouped bar chart showcases the most popular fields of study among Australians by gender, providing a breakdown of fields of study in higher education ordered in total % across all genders.

The most popular fields of study are Society, Culture & Management and Commerce fields, whereas Agriculture & Food/Hospitality related degrees are less common.
